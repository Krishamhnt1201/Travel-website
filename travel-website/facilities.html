Travel Essentials

Transportation and Currency Guide for France, USA, Japan, and Switzerland

Planning your trip? Here's a focused overview of key facilities for getting around in Paris (France), New York City (USA), Tokyo (Japan), and Zurich (Switzerland). We've highlighted reliable car rental agencies, efficient public transportation options, and the local currency—essential for seamless 2025 travels.

France: Paris – Navigating the City of Light
Renting a car is ideal for exploring beyond Paris, like the Loire Valley, but note traffic and parking challenges in the city.
Car Rental Agencies
Europcar
Widespread locations including CDG Airport; economy cars from €25/day, with electric vehicle rentals and one-way drop-offs available.
Sixt
Premium fleet with SUVs from €40/day; English support and add-ons like GPS.
Budget
Affordable with 440+ pickup spots; last-minute deals starting at €30/day.
Public Transportation
Metro and RER
16 Metro lines plus 5 RER express trains cover the city and suburbs; frequent (every 2-5 minutes peak), with stations ~500m apart.
Buses and Trams
347 bus lines and 14 tram lines; night buses run weekends; single ticket €2.10 (or €2.50 onboard).
Passes
Navigo Découverte card or app-loaded tickets for unlimited rides (e.g., €30/day pass).
Currency Used
Euro (EUR) – €1 ≈ $1.10 USD (as of Sept 2025). Cards widely accepted; ATMs abundant.
USA: New York City – The Pulse of Urban Mobility
NYC traffic is intense, so rentals suit day trips to nearby areas like the Hamptons. Airport pickups are easiest.
Car Rental Agencies
Enterprise Rent-A-Car
Neighborhood branches and airports; midsize from $40/day, with free pickups.
Sixt
Premium options like SUVs from $35/day; add-ons include child seats and Wi-Fi.
Hertz
Vast network; economy cars from $33/day, app-based bookings.
Public Transportation
Subway and Buses
472 stations across 5 boroughs; trains every 2-10 minutes; buses accessible with low floors.
Ferries and Staten Island Ferry
Free scenic rides; NYC Ferry for $4/trip to Manhattan hotspots.
Passes
OMNY contactless or MetroCard (phasing out by end-2025); $2.90/ride or $34 weekly unlimited.
Currency Used
United States Dollar (USD) – Standard global reserve; $1 USD. Digital payments dominant.
Japan: Tokyo – Precision and Punctuality
Drive on the left; rentals best for rural escapes from Tokyo. International license required.
Car Rental Agencies
Toyota Rent a Car
Extensive English GPS-equipped fleet; compacts from ¥6,000/day (~$40 USD).
ORIX Rent a Car
Airport hubs with hybrids; from ¥7,000/day, including insurance.
Times CAR RENTAL
24/7 multilingual support; family vans from ¥5,000/day.
Public Transportation
JR Lines and Subway
Yamanote Loop connects key areas; Tokyo Metro (9 lines) and Toei (4 lines) for underground efficiency.
Buses and Waterbuses
Scenic Sumida River options; buses supplement trains in outer areas.
Passes
IC cards like Suica/Pasmo for tap-and-go (¥500 deposit); ¥1,000/day unlimited via Tokyo Metro 24-hour ticket.
Currency Used
Japanese Yen (JPY) – ¥1 ≈ $0.007 USD (as of Sept 2025). Cash preferred in small shops; IC cards for transit.
Switzerland: Zurich – Efficient Alpine Access
Vignette (highway sticker) mandatory; pristine roads make driving scenic.
Car Rental Agencies
Europcar
City and airport locations; economy from CHF 45/day (~$52 USD).
Sixt
Luxury and EVs from CHF 40/day; English bookings.
Hertz
Reliable with add-ons; compacts from CHF 35/day.
Public Transportation
Trams and Buses
15 tram lines and extensive buses; every 5-10 minutes, fully accessible.
S-Bahn Trains
Regional connections; Zurich HB station is Europe's busiest.
Passes
ZVV day pass CHF 10.80 for city zone; Swiss Travel Pass for nationwide (from CHF 232/3 days).
Currency Used
Swiss Franc (CHF) – CHF 1 ≈ $1.15 USD (as of Sept 2025). Euros accepted in tourist spots; contactless common.
